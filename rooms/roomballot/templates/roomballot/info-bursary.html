Bursaries & Rent Reduction

There are a range of ways that College reduces your rent, and a range of bursaries that you can apply for if the cost of rent is too high. We are currently working with college to make these more transparent and open - more details of which will be released with finalised rents next week.

College Applied Rebate

As part of the agreement for the college to implement 38 week rent contracts it was agreed that some students would be given a rent rebate depending on their parental household income (as assessed by HEBSS data).  The rebate is due if household income falls between £40,000 and £62,143.  The rebate is included on the Lent Term college bill. The College must have the information from HEBSS (the Higher Education Bursary and Scholarship Scheme). This means that, for College to have your information, you must allow the Student Loan Company to pass on your household financial information to the University. If you think you fall into this category, make sure to do this next year when you apply for your student finance.

NEW - we have introduced a new fixed rebate of £300, which is payable if your household income falls into the bracket above and you pick a room in Band 4 or below. More information about this new bursary is available here.

Hardship Grant

This is a grant that you can apply to if the cost of accommodation is too severe. While not guaranteed, any good reason will usually result in a pay-out. Importantly, there is no strict parameter on hardship – so things like 'your only option was to take an expensive room' could be covered.
For more guidance on this please get in touch with Tutorials and Admissions.

Talk to your Tutor

If you are having problems affording rent, make clear to your Tutor that this is the case, and ask if there are any other options. It is important College is aware of your issues, as Cambridge University guarantees that nobody will have to leave the University simply because of the cost of living here. They may be able to provide other options that are not available here, or refer you to the Senior Tutor on the matter.
