Ballot Information

The ballot will be held on the weekend of {{ date }}. Second years will pick on the Saturday, and first years will pick on the Sunday. Balloting will begin at 9am, and you'll be allocated a 5-minute slot in which to pick your room.

The room ballot is an ordered list of first and second year students - where your position in the ballot reflects the order in which you will choose a room on ballot weekend. The ballot occurs over two days, in which second years take priority and choose first, followed by the freshers. Within your half of the ballot, your position is randomly determined (there's no priority for scholars etc). When you ballot again in your second year, the order of your year will be reversed. So if you were at the bottom of the ballot in first year, you will be at the very top in second year.

A set of rooms is reserved out of the ballot and can be applied for via the Tutorial & Admissions Office by students with exceptional requirements - these tend to be upon referral from the DRC or with medical evidence. All students who come under this remit should have been contacted by T&A. If you have not been contacted but feel you should have, please email the college academic registrar, Sarah Pickard (sjp54@cam.ac.uk).
You can feature on the ballot in two ways: either a individual or as part of a syndicate. A syndicate is a way for a group of people (Maximum 6 people) can choose rooms at the same time as each other. If you want to live with a certain group of people next year, being in a syndicate with them is a way to make that more likely because you can easily coordinate what you pick when it's your turn on ballot weekend. Being in a syndicate does not mean that you have to live with the people in it - it merely makes the option of living with them easier.
Talk to your friends. It is actually quite a big decision so do give it some serious thought, but remember that it's also fine to not be in a syndicate. When you're ready, log on to the Balloting System to form your syndicates. Only freshers need to form syndicates, and you will have until {{ date }} to complete this.
